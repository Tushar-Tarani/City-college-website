1. The Core Philosophy (Overview & Leadership)

  • About the Department:

    • Welcome to the Department of Electrical Engineering at City College of Engineering. Established to drive the future of energy and power, our department focuses on the fundamental principles and advanced applications of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. We equip students with the technical prowess needed to design sustainable power grids, develop electric vehicle infrastructures, and manage complex industrial control systems.


  • Vision:

    • To be a premier center of excellence in Electrical Engineering education and research, producing visionary engineers who lead the transition toward sustainable energy and smart electrical systems.


  • Mission:

    1. To deliver a robust curriculum that balances fundamental circuit theory with modern advancements in power electronics and renewable energy.
    2. To foster hands-on learning through high-voltage laboratories and real-world electrical simulation projects.
    3. To promote innovative research in smart grids, energy conservation, and electric mobility.
    4. To build strategic partnerships with the power, manufacturing, and automation industries to ensure graduates are highly employable.

2. Academic Programs & Specializations

  • Programs Offered:

    1. B.Tech / B.E. in Electrical Engineering (4-Year Undergraduate Program)
    2. M.Tech / M.E. in Power Systems / Power Electronics (2-Year Postgraduate Program)
    3. Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering (Doctoral Research Program)



  • Core Curriculum Focus:

    • Our curriculum is structured to build a deep understanding of electrical systems. Key core areas include Circuit Theory and Analysis, Electrical Machines, Power Systems, Control Systems, and Electromagnetic Fields.


  • Project-Based Learning:

    • We bridge theory and practice. Every semester includes hands-on circuit building and simulation, culminating in a final-year capstone project where students design working power converters, energy management systems, or renewable energy integration models.

3. Infrastructure & Facilities

  • State-of-the-Art Laboratories:

    • Our department features highly specialized spaces designed for comprehensive electrical testing and safety:
    1. Electrical Machines Lab: Equipped with transformers, induction motors, and synchronous generators for comprehensive load and performance testing.
    2. Power Electronics & Drives Lab: A dedicated space for testing inverters, converters, and modern motor control drives.
    3. Power Systems & High Voltage Lab: Geared toward simulating transmission line models, switchgear protection, and high-voltage insulation testing.
    4. Control Systems & Automation Lab: Featuring PLC hardware, SCADA systems, and microcontrollers for industrial process automation.



  • Library & Resources:

    • The department maintains a dedicated resource center with comprehensive access to the IEEE Power & Energy Society (PES) journals, national electrical codes, and advanced research papers.
