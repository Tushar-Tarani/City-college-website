1. The Core Philosophy (Overview & Leadership)

  • About the Department:

    • Welcome to the Department of Automation and Robotics at City college of Engineering. Established in 1999, our department is dedicated to the intelligent integration of mechanical, electrical, and computer systems. We focus on equipping students with the skills to design, build, and deploy autonomous systems, industrial robots, and smart manufacturing technologies that are shaping the future of Industry 4.0.


  • Vision:

    • To be a globally recognized center of excellence in Automation and Robotics education, research, and innovation, producing engineers who lead the transition towards intelligent and autonomous systems.


  • Mission:

    1. To deliver a multidisciplinary curriculum blending mechanics, electronics, and artificial intelligence.
    2. To foster hands-on learning through advanced robotics labs and industry-driven physical projects.
    3. To promote research in autonomous vehicles, industrial automation, and human-robot interaction.
    4. To build strong partnerships with manufacturing and tech industries to ensure our graduates are future-ready.



  • Message from the Head of Department:

2. Academic Programs & Specializations

  • Programs Offered:

    1. B.Tech / B.E. in Automation and Robotics (4-Year Undergraduate Program)
    2. M.Tech / M.E. in Robotics and Industrial Automation (2-Year Postgraduate Program)
    3. Ph.D. in Robotics and Industrial Automation (Doctoral Research Program)



  • Core Curriculum Focus:

    • Our curriculum bridges multiple engineering disciplines. Key core areas include Kinematics and Dynamics of Machinery, Control Systems, Microprocessors and Microcontrollers, Sensors and Actuators, and Robot Programming.


  • Project-Based Learning:

    • We emphasize creating physical, working prototypes. Every semester involves hands-on hardware projects, culminating in a final-year capstone where students design fully functional automated systems or robots to solve real-world industrial or societal problems.

3. Infrastructure & Facilities

  • State-of-the-Art Laboratories:

    • Our department houses highly specialized spaces designed for hands-on creation:
    1. Industrial Robotics Lab: Equipped with industrial robotic arms (like KUKA, ABB, or FANUC) for pick-and-place, welding, and assembly simulations.
    2. Mechatronics & Control Lab: Dedicated to PLCs (Programmable Logic Controllers), SCADA systems, and pneumatics/hydraulics training.
    3. Autonomous Systems Lab: Geared toward drone development, automated guided vehicles (AGVs), and LIDAR-based navigation testing.
    4. Fabrication & Maker Space: Featuring 3D printers, CNC machines, and PCB fabrication tools for rapid prototyping of custom robot parts.



  • Library & Resources:

    • Beyond the central college library, the department provides access to leading research materials via the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society, along with a dedicated repository of mechanical and electronic components for student projects.

4. Industry Connect & Placements

  • Top Recruiters:

    • Our graduates are highly sought after by core manufacturing, automotive, and tech companies. We proudly place students in leading corporations including Tesla, Siemens, Bosch, Tata Motors, Rockwell Automation as well as high-growth robotics startups.


  • Internship Opportunities:

    • Our dedicated placement cell ensures students gain practical floor experience through mandatory e.g., 6-month internships in manufacturing plants, R&D labs, or specialized automation firms.


  • Alumni Network:

    • Our alumni work globally in sectors ranging from automotive to aerospace. They actively return to mentor current students, offer workshops on the latest automation trends, and provide pathways for campus recruitment.
