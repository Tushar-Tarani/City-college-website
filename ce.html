1. The Core Philosophy (Overview & Leadership)

    • Welcome to the Department of Civil Engineering at City College of Engineering. Established to build the foundations of modern society, our department is dedicated to the design, construction, and maintenance of the physical and naturally built environment. We focus on equipping students with the expertise to develop sustainable smart cities, resilient infrastructure, and innovative environmental solutions.

    • To be a premier center of excellence in Civil Engineering education and research, producing visionary engineers who design, build, and sustain resilient and eco-friendly infrastructure for a global society.

2. Academic Programs & Specializations

  • Programs Offered:

    1. B.Tech / B.E. in Civil Engineering (4-Year Undergraduate Program)
    2. M.Tech / M.E. in Structural Engineering / Construction Management (2-Year Postgraduate Program)
    3. Ph.D. in Civil Engineering (Doctoral Research Program)



  • Core Curriculum Focus:

    • Our curriculum is structured to build a deep understanding of structural and environmental mechanics. Key core areas include Engineering Mechanics, Strength of Materials, Structural Analysis, Geotechnical Engineering (Soil Mechanics), Fluid Mechanics, and Transportation Engineering.


  • Project-Based Learning:

    • We bridge classroom theory with the construction site. Every semester includes hands-on drafting and material testing, culminating in a final-year capstone project where students develop comprehensive structural designs, urban town planning blueprints, or environmental impact assessments.

3. Infrastructure & Facilities

  • State-of-the-Art Laboratories:

    • Our department features expansive laboratories designed for heavy-duty material testing and environmental analysis:
    1. Concrete Technology & Highway Engineering Lab: Equipped with compression testing machines, bitumen extractors, and curing tanks for advanced material strength analysis.
    2. Geotechnical Engineering Lab: A dedicated space for soil testing, featuring triaxial shear test apparatus and consolidation testing setups.
    3. Environmental Engineering Lab: Geared toward testing water quality, wastewater treatment processes, and air pollution metrics.
    4. Advanced Surveying & GIS Lab: Featuring modern Total Stations, digital theodolites, Auto-levels, and GPS mapping equipment for precise fieldwork.



  • Library & Resources:

    • The department maintains a dedicated resource center with comprehensive access to ASCE (American Society of Civil Engineers) journals, standard IS (Indian Standard) Codes, and detailed material catalogs used in the field.
